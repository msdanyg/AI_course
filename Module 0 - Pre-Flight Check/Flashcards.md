# Module 0 Flashcards
## Pre-Flight Check: Setup, Policy & Boundaries

---

### Card 1

**Front:**
What are the four Squadron roles, and what tools might fill each?

**Back:**
1. **Mission Control (Reasoning)**: Claude, ChatGPT, Claude Code, Cursor
2. **Recon & Radar (Research)**: Gemini, Bing AI, Google NotebookLM
3. **Flight Recorder (Capture)**: Granola, Chorus.ai, Zoom AI
4. **Specialized Units**: Figma AI, Zendesk AI, ChurnZero, etc.

*Your Squadron depends on what tools you have access to. Think in roles, not specific tools.*

---

### Card 2

**Front:**
What are the three zones in the Traffic Light Protocol?

**Back:**
**Green (Go Freely):** Customer data, internal docs, usage analytics, business communications. Use freely for analysis and drafting.

**Yellow (Review Required):** Marketing collateral with PII, competitive intel with sources, NDA data. May need PR/Legal review before external distribution.

**Red (Never Share):** API keys, passwords, credentials, security configurations. Never enter into any AI tool.

---

### Card 3

**Front:**
What is the core principle of AI data safety at ActivTrak?

**Back:**
**"AI drafts. Humans send."**

The risk is not in analyzing data—it's in sharing unreviewed AI output externally.

You clear missions for takeoff and own what leaves the runway. Always review AI outputs before sending to anyone outside your team.

---

### Card 4

**Front:**
What's the difference between Solo Pilot and Squadron Leader thinking?

**Back:**
**Solo Pilot:**
- Minimal context ("Write me an email")
- Freeform questions
- New chat for everything
- Copy-paste outputs directly

**Squadron Leader:**
- Rich context (background, goals, constraints)
- Structured requests with clear outputs
- Persistent projects and workflows
- Verify, refine, then use

---

### Card 5

**Front:**
When should you use Mission Control tools vs. Recon & Radar tools?

**Back:**
**Use Mission Control (Claude, ChatGPT, etc.) for:**
- Strategic analysis and reasoning
- Writing and editing content
- Code execution and data analysis
- Synthesizing information you provide

**Use Recon & Radar (Gemini, Bing AI, etc.) for:**
- Real-time web research
- Current competitor information
- Fact verification and grounding
- Searching documents and workspaces

*Mission Control works with what you give it. Recon & Radar fetches what's out there.*

---

### Card 6

**Front:**
What four questions should you ask before pasting data into an AI tool?

**Back:**
1. **What is this data?** (Customer info? Internal doc? Credential?)
2. **What zone is it in?** (Green/Yellow/Red)
3. **What will I do with the output?** (Internal analysis? External communication?)
4. **Have I reviewed before sending?** (Never auto-send AI outputs externally)

*If Green + reviewing outputs = proceed confidently*

---

### Card 7

**Front:**
What does "Insights, Not Oversight" mean for your AI usage?

**Back:**
ActivTrak's philosophy is about helping organizations understand work patterns without creating surveillance culture.

Your AI usage should reflect this:
- Use AI to generate genuine insights, not busywork
- Focus on understanding patterns, not just producing output
- Prioritize quality over quantity

*Squadron Leaders use AI purposefully, not just to generate content.*
