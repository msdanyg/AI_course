Why Think in Roles?

Your Squadron depends on what tools you have access to. You don't need every tool—you need to understand what roles your tools fill and how to orchestrate them effectively.

Scenario Squadron Role Example Tools
Draft a competitive positioning email Mission Control Claude, ChatGPT
Check competitor's current pricing Recon & Radar Gemini, Perplexity
Summarize yesterday's client call Flight Recorder → Mission Control Granola/Chorus → Claude
Analyze a spreadsheet of account data Mission Control Claude, ChatGPT
Find a document in Google Drive Recon & Radar Gemini

The Squadron Leader Difference

The Squadron Leader thinks in roles and matches tasks to capabilities. The Solo Pilot uses whatever's in front of them without considering fit.

The Traffic Light Protocol

The most important concept in this module: what you can share with AI tools.

The Key Insight: Input vs. Output

The risk is rarely in analyzing data. The risk is in sharing unreviewed AI output.

"AI drafts. Humans send."

You clear missions for takeoff. You own what leaves the runway.

The Three Zones

🟢 Green: Go Freely

  • Customer/Account information
  • Internal documents
  • Usage data and analytics
  • Business communications

Use freely for analysis. Review outputs before external sharing.

🟡 Yellow: Review Required

  • Marketing with customer PII
  • Competitive intel with sources
  • Third-party NDA data

Can analyze, but outputs may need PR/Legal review.

🔴 Red: Never Share

  • API keys and passwords
  • Admin credentials
  • Security configurations

Never enter into any AI tool.

Decision Framework

When you're about to paste something into an AI tool, ask:

  1. What is this data? (Customer info? Internal doc? Credential?)
  2. What zone is it in? (Green/Yellow/Red)
  3. What will I do with the output? (Internal analysis? External communication?)
  4. Have I reviewed before sending? (Never auto-send AI outputs externally)

If it's Green and you're reviewing outputs:

Proceed confidently. This is exactly how you should use AI to accelerate your work.

Setting Up Your Cockpit

Your cockpit will look different from your colleagues'—that's expected. Let's identify what you have and verify it's working.

Step 1: Inventory Your Tools

First, identify what tools you have for each Squadron role:

Squadron Role Do You Have?
Mission Control (Reasoning) Claude? ChatGPT? Claude Code? Cursor?
Recon & Radar (Research) Gemini? Perplexity? NotebookLM?
Flight Recorder (Capture) Granola? Chorus.ai? Zoom AI?
Specialized Units Figma AI? Zendesk AI? Others?

Step 2: Configure Your Tools

For each tool you identified, verify it's working:

  1. Log in to the tool
  2. Verify your account is active (look for enterprise features)
  3. Test with a simple prompt
  4. Test file upload if available
