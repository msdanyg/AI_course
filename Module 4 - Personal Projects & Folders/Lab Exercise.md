## Step 1: Create the Project (2 minutes)

1. Open [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) in your browser
2. Click **"Projects"** in the left sidebar
3. Click **"New Project"**
4. Name your Project: **"LinkedIn Content Studio"**
5. Click **"Create Project"**

**✅ CHECKPOINT:** You should now see your new Project in the sidebar. Click into it to continue.

---

## Step 2: Write Custom Instructions (5-7 minutes)

**Your mission:** Write "Standing Orders" that apply to every LinkedIn post drafted in this Project.

1. In your new Project, scroll down to the **"Custom Instructions"** section
2. Write instructions that tell Claude how to help you create LinkedIn posts

### Tips for Writing Effective Custom Instructions

**Start with 4 key sections:**

1. **ROLE:** Who are you? What's your job title and company?
2. **CONTEXT:** What does your company do? What's your positioning or philosophy?
3. **OUTPUT RULES:** How should posts be structured? (length, format, tone)
4. **NEVER:** What should Claude absolutely avoid? (forbidden language, bad patterns)

**Best practices:**

- ✅ **Be specific:** "150-250 words, 3-5 short paragraphs" beats "keep it short"
- ✅ **Use examples:** "Say 'reduced conflicts by 40%' not 'improved efficiency'"
- ✅ **List forbidden terms explicitly:** "NEVER use 'synergy,' 'leverage,' 'disrupt'"
- ✅ **Start with 200 words**—you can always add more later
- ✅ **Put critical rules at the END**—Claude pays more attention to instructions at the bottom
- ❌ **Don't write 2,000 words** covering every edge case (Claude ignores over-instruction)

<details>
<summary><strong>💡 Show me an example structure</strong></summary>

```
ROLE: You are my LinkedIn content assistant. I'm a [Your Job Title] at ActivTrak, a workforce analytics company.

YOUR MISSION:
- Draft engaging LinkedIn posts that sound like me, not corporate marketing
- Highlight real business problems and how we solve them
- Use storytelling and examples, not just product features
- Keep posts authentic, conversational, and value-focused

POST STRUCTURE:
1. Hook (1-2 sentences that grab attention)
2. Body (problem, insight, or story)
3. CTA (comment prompt or link)

LENGTH & FORMAT:
- Target 150-250 words (3-5 short paragraphs)
- Use line breaks for readability (1-2 sentences per paragraph)
- No emojis unless I specifically request them
- End with a question or CTA to drive engagement

VOICE & TONE:
- Professional but approachable—like explaining to a colleague over coffee
- Avoid buzzwords ("synergy," "leverage," "disrupt")
- Use "we" when talking about ActivTrak, "I" when sharing my perspective
- Be specific with data: "reduced scheduling conflicts by 40%" beats "improved efficiency"

NEVER:
- Use surveillance language ("monitor employees," "track workers")
- Write generic LinkedIn fluff ("Excited to share," "Thrilled to announce")
- Include hashtags unless I ask for them
```

</details>

3. Click **"Save"** or confirm changes

**✅ CHECKPOINT:** Your custom instructions are saved. You should see them displayed in the Project settings.

## Step 3 (Optional): Add Reference Documents (3-5 minutes)

If you have past LinkedIn posts or brand voice guidelines, you can upload them to help Claude match your style:

**Good candidates:**
- Past LinkedIn posts that performed well (export as PDF or text)
- LinkedIn-specific brand voice guidelines (tone, terminology, what to avoid)
- Key messaging documents

**How to upload:**
1. Click **"Add Content"** in the Project interface
2. Click **"Upload Files"**
3. Select 1-3 documents from your local files
4. Wait for the processing indicator

**💡 PRO TIP:** Use Markdown (.md) or plain text (.txt) files when possible—they work better than PDFs for Claude's retrieval system.

---

## Step 4: Test Your Project (3-5 minutes)

**Your mission:** Verify the Project works by creating a real LinkedIn post.

1. Click **"Start a new chat"** within your Project
2. Type a prompt like this:

   > "Draft a LinkedIn post about [a topic you find interesting related to ActivTrak—flexible work schedules, hybrid teams, productivity coaching, schedule adherence, etc.]"

3. **Review Claude's output:**
   - ✅ Does it remember your role and company without you stating it?
   - ✅ Is the post 150-250 words with short paragraphs?
   - ✅ Does it avoid buzzwords and generic LinkedIn fluff?
   - ✅ Does it sound conversational and authentic?
   - ✅ Does it end with a question or CTA?

4. If something doesn't match your instructions, go back and make them more explicit

**✅ CHECKPOINT:** Your Claude Project successfully created a LinkedIn post that follows your instructions.
