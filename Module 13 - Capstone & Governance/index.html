The Governance Framework

The foundation for sustainable AI usage

The Three Pillars

Before your capstone project, let's establish the governance principles that make everything else sustainable.

🎯

Accountability

"AI drafts. Humans send." You own every output that bears your name.

🔍

Verification

Scale scrutiny with consequence. Different outputs need different review levels.

💡

Transparency

Know what context you provided, how the AI reasoned, and its limitations.

The "Send" Button is Yours

When you use AI to draft an email, analyze data, or create a presentation—the AI is your collaborator. But you are the author. You own the consequences. "The AI wrote it" is never a valid excuse.

Verification Standards by Risk Level

Output Type Risk Level Verification Standard
Internal brainstorming Low Quick review for relevance
Draft internal documents Low-Medium Review for accuracy and tone
Customer-facing emails Medium Full review + fact-check key claims
Data analysis conclusions Medium-High Verify methodology + spot-check calculations
Public content High Multi-person review + PR/Legal if needed
Financial/legal documents High Expert review + compliance check

The Spot-Check Protocol

For data analysis, don't verify every number—use this protocol:

  1. Methodology: Did the AI approach the problem correctly?
  2. Spot-check: Manually verify 3-5 data points
  3. Logic: Do conclusions follow from the data?
  4. Edge cases: Test unusual scenarios

If all pass, trust the output. If any fails, investigate.

Measuring Your ROI

Is all this worth it? Let's find out.

The Time-Quality Matrix

AI impact falls into four quadrants:

Lower Quality Same/Higher Quality
Faster Risky Acceleration The Sweet Spot
Same Speed Net Negative Modest Win

Net Time Saved Formula

Net Time Saved = (Time task would have taken) - (Time with AI + Review time + Fix time)

For most tasks, Squadron Leaders see 30-70% savings. Some tasks see 90%+. A few see no savings at all.

Quality Gains

Time isn't everything. AI often improves quality in ways harder to measure:

  • Comprehensiveness: Covering angles you would have missed
  • Consistency: Maintaining voice/format across documents
  • Creativity: Generating options you wouldn't have considered
  • Accuracy: Catching errors through computational analysis

The Monthly Review

Every month, reflect on five questions:

  1. Volume: What percentage of my work involved AI?
  2. Time: Where did I save the most time?
  3. Quality: Where did AI most improve my output?
  4. Struggles: Where did AI add friction?
  5. Learning: What new capability should I develop?

Squadron Leaders who review monthly improve dramatically faster than those who don't.

Capstone Project

The Squadron Mission (45-60 minutes)

1

Phase 1: Capture

Voice/notes to XML context

Create source material about a real business challenge you face:

  • Record a 2-3 minute voice memo OR pull existing meeting notes
  • Capture: the problem, stakeholders, constraints, success criteria
  • Structure it in XML for Mission Control
<context>
<source>[Voice memo / Meeting notes]</source>
<problem>[Your challenge in 2-3 sentences]</problem>
<stakeholders>[Who cares? What are their concerns?]</stakeholders>
<constraints>[Budget, timeline, resource limitations]</constraints>
<success_criteria>[How will we know if this succeeds?]</success_criteria>
</context>

✓
I've structured it in XML
2

Phase 2: Research

Recon & Radar to Fact Brief

Use Gemini or your Recon tool to gather verified facts:

  • Identify 3+ research needs for your challenge
  • Search for current data and authoritative sources
  • Create a Fact Brief with citations

✓

Phase 3: Analysis

Code/logic to insights

Conduct systematic analysis:

  • If data-based: Use code execution for calculations and visualizations
  • If logical: Systematically compare options with clear criteria
  • Document your methodology so you can verify it
