## SECTION 1: THE GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORK (3 minutes)

**[SCREEN: "The Three Pillars of AI Governance"]**

**INSTRUCTOR (V.O.):**
"Before your capstone project, let's establish the governance principles that make everything else sustainable. This isn't bureaucracy—it's what allows you to move fast with confidence.

**[SCREEN: First pillar - "Accountability"]**

The first pillar is Accountability. We've said it throughout this course: AI drafts, humans send. But let me be more direct now.

**[SCREEN: Quote - "The 'Send' Button is Yours"]**

When you use AI to draft an email, analyze data, or create a presentation—the AI is your collaborator. But you are the author. You own the consequences. 'The AI wrote it' is never a valid excuse. Your signature goes on everything.

**[SCREEN: Second pillar - "Verification"]**

The second pillar is Verification. Not all outputs need the same scrutiny.

**[SCREEN: Verification matrix showing risk levels]**

Internal brainstorming? Quick review. Customer-facing emails? Full review plus fact-checking. Financial documents? Expert review plus compliance check.

**[SCREEN: "The Spot-Check Protocol"]**

For data analysis, use the spot-check protocol. Verify the methodology is sound. Manually check three to five data points. Confirm conclusions follow logically. Test edge cases. If it passes these checks, you can trust the output.

**[SCREEN: Third pillar - "Transparency"]**

The third pillar is Transparency. Know what you're working with.

**[SCREEN: Three questions - "What the AI knows / How it reasoned / What it cannot do"]**

What context did you provide—and what gaps might the AI be filling with assumptions? For complex decisions, review the Extended Thinking. Ask the AI to explain its reasoning. And remember what it cannot do: access real-time information without Recon & Radar, verify its own claims, or know if its training data is outdated."

## SECTION 2: MEASURING YOUR ROI (2 minutes)

**[SCREEN: "Measuring Your ROI"]**

**INSTRUCTOR (V.O.):**
"You've invested significant time learning these skills. Let's talk about measuring the return on that investment.

**[SCREEN: The Time-Quality Matrix - 2x2 grid]**

AI impact falls into four quadrants. You want to be in the sweet spot: same or better quality in less time. That's where Squadron Leaders operate.

**[SCREEN: "Net Time Saved" formula]**

Be honest about time saved. It's the time the task would have taken, minus time with AI, minus review time, minus time fixing mistakes. For most tasks, you'll see thirty to seventy percent savings. Some tasks see ninety percent plus. A few see no savings at all.

**[SCREEN: "Quality Gained" metrics]**

But time isn't everything. AI often improves quality in ways that are harder to measure. Comprehensiveness—covering angles you would have missed. Consistency across documents. Creativity in generating options. Accuracy through computational analysis.

**[SCREEN: "Monthly Review" checklist]**

Every month, reflect: What percentage of your work involved AI? Where did you save the most time? Where did it most improve quality? Where did it add friction? What should you learn next? Squadron Leaders who review monthly improve dramatically faster than those who don't."

## SECTION 3: THE CAPSTONE PROJECT (4 minutes)

**[SCREEN: "The Squadron Mission - Capstone Project"]**

**INSTRUCTOR (V.O.):**
"The capstone project is called The Squadron Mission. You'll execute a comprehensive workflow demonstrating mastery across all modules.

**[SCREEN: Mission brief summary]**

Your mission: prepare a strategic recommendation for your team. A one-page brief that addresses a real business challenge, grounded in verified facts, with data analysis where relevant, following your organization's format, ready for external distribution.

**[SCREEN: Five phase workflow diagram]**

The mission has five required phases.

**[SCREEN: Phase 1 - Capture]**

Phase 1: Capture. Start with voice or meeting context—a recorded memo or existing meeting notes. Transfer it to Mission Control using proper XML structure. This proves you understand how Flight Recorder feeds the Squadron.

**[SCREEN: Phase 2 - Research]**

Phase 2: Research. Use Recon & Radar to ground your work in verified facts. Gather current information. Verify claims. Document sources. Create a Fact Brief with citations. This proves you can prevent hallucination through grounding.

**[SCREEN: Phase 3 - Analysis]**

Phase 3: Analysis. If your challenge involves data, use code execution. Calculate metrics with Python. Generate visualizations. Document your methodology so you can verify it. This proves you understand the calculator problem and its solution.

**[SCREEN: Phase 4 - Draft]**

Phase 4: Draft. Create your deliverable using a structured prompt. Role, context, facts, analysis, instructions, format. This proves you've mastered prompt architecture.

**[SCREEN: Phase 5 - Verify]**

Phase 5: Verify. Apply the Human-in-the-Loop standard. Accuracy check: facts verified, numbers spot-checked, no hallucination. Quality check: recommendation clear, evidence compelling, tone appropriate. Governance check: no Red zone data, Yellow zone reviewed. And run a Red Team check—ask the AI to find weaknesses in your own brief.

**[SCREEN: Complete workflow visualization]**

When you complete all five phases, you've demonstrated end-to-end Squadron capability."
