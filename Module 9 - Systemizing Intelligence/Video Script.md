# Module 8 Video Script
## Systemizing Intelligence: Projects, Skills, Gems & Custom GPTs

**Duration:** 8-10 minutes
**Format:** Screen share with instructor voice-over
**Tone:** Practical, empowering, focused on building

---

## OPENING (45 seconds)

**[SCREEN: Title card - "Module 8: Systemizing Intelligence"]**

**INSTRUCTOR (V.O.):**
"You've spent seven modules learning to fly. You can capture intelligence anywhere, process it with reasoning engines, verify with grounded research, and deliver to collaboration tools.

But let me ask you something uncomfortable:

**[SCREEN: Animation - Clock showing repeated setup time]**

How much time do you spend every conversation explaining who you are, what you do, and how you want outputs formatted?

That's the repetition tax. And in this module, we eliminate it.

**[SCREEN: Text overlay - "From Squadron Leader to Squadron Architect"]**

You're about to become a Squadron Architect—someone who builds persistent systems that work for you even when you're not actively prompting."

---

## SECTION 1: THE PROBLEM (1 minute)

**[SCREEN: Animation - Blank conversation starting fresh]**

**INSTRUCTOR (V.O.):**
"Every AI conversation starts with amnesia. Claude doesn't remember your last chat. It doesn't know your preferences, your company, or your terminology.

This creates three problems:

**[SCREEN: Animated list appearing]**

First: Repetition tax. You spend the first few exchanges rebuilding context every single time.

Second: Consistency drift. Without persistent instructions, your outputs vary based on how you phrased things that day.

Third: Expertise ceiling. You can't build on previous work. Every conversation starts at zero.

**[SCREEN: Four platform logos - Claude Projects, Skills, Gemini Gems, Custom GPTs]**

The solution? Persistent context systems. And there are four you need to know."

---

## SECTION 2: CLAUDE PROJECTS (2 minutes)

**[SCREEN: Claude interface - Projects panel]**

**INSTRUCTOR (V.O.):**
"Let's start with the most powerful: Claude Projects.

A Project is a persistent workspace with three components:

**[SCREEN: Visual diagram showing three elements]**

Custom instructions—how Claude should behave in this context.
Knowledge files—documents Claude can reference.
Conversation history—past chats that inform future responses.

Think of it as giving Claude a dedicated office for a specific mission.

**[SCREEN: Demo - Creating a new Project]**

Let me show you how to build one. I'm creating a 'Customer Intelligence Hub.'

First, I write custom instructions. Notice I'm specific about context, behavior, and output preferences.

**[SCREEN: Highlighting key instruction elements]**

I tell Claude my role, my key accounts, and my terminology preferences. I specify how I want outputs formatted—traffic lights for health status, bullet points not paragraphs.

**[SCREEN: Uploading files to Project]**

Next, I upload knowledge files. Account profiles, QBR notes, product documentation. Claude can now reference these in every conversation.

**[SCREEN: Starting conversation in Project]**

Now when I ask about an account, Claude already knows the context. No setup required.

**[SCREEN: Comparison - before/after]**

This is the power of Projects. Same question, but with context built in."

---

## SECTION 3: CLAUDE SKILLS (1.5 minutes)

**[SCREEN: Skills interface]**

**INSTRUCTOR (V.O.):**
"While Projects provide persistent context, Skills provide portable methodology. They're reusable prompt templates you can invoke with a trigger phrase.

**[SCREEN: Example Skill being created]**

Here's one I use constantly: 'Quick comp analysis.'

When I type 'Quick comp on' followed by a company name, Claude automatically runs this structured analysis: positioning, strengths, weaknesses, counter-positioning, and verification flags.

**[SCREEN: Skill being invoked]**

Watch. 'Quick comp on Hubstaff.'

**[SCREEN: Structured output appearing]**

Same methodology, every time. Consistent, efficient, no explanation needed.

**[SCREEN: Comparison table - Projects vs Skills]**

Here's how to choose: Projects for ongoing work with documents. Skills for repeatable tasks you use across different contexts.

Best practice? Combine them. Use Skills inside Projects for maximum efficiency."

---

## SECTION 4: GEMINI GEMS (1.5 minutes)

**[SCREEN: Gemini Gems interface]**

**INSTRUCTOR (V.O.):**
"Gemini Gems are custom AI assistants optimized for Google Workspace integration. Think of them as specialized reconnaissance units.

**[SCREEN: Creating a Gem]**

Let me build a 'Market Intel' Gem. I give it a name, then write instructions focusing on competitive research.

**[SCREEN: Highlighting Gem configuration]**

I specify my industry focus, key competitors, and research standards. I tell it to verify claims, include dates, and flag outdated information.

**[SCREEN: Gem in action with web results]**

Now when I use this Gem, it searches with my parameters built in. It knows to look for workforce analytics competitors, to verify sources, to flag old data.

**[SCREEN: Google Workspace integration demo]**

And here's where Gems shine: native Google integration. I can export directly to Docs, pull data into Sheets, generate Slides content.

**[SCREEN: Decision diagram]**

Use Gems when you need real-time research, Google Workspace integration, or when you're working with current information that Claude's training might miss."

---

## SECTION 5: CUSTOM GPTs (1.5 minutes)

**[SCREEN: ChatGPT Custom GPT builder]**

**INSTRUCTOR (V.O.):**
"The fourth pillar: Custom GPTs. These are persistent assistants in ChatGPT that you can share with others.

**[SCREEN: GPT configuration screen]**

I'm building a 'Battle Card Generator' for our sales team. I give it a name, description, and detailed instructions.

**[SCREEN: Uploading knowledge to GPT]**

I upload competitive intelligence files, positioning documents, objection handling guides.

**[SCREEN: Setting capabilities]**

I enable browsing for current research, disable DALL-E since we don't need images.

**[SCREEN: GPT being shared]**

Here's the key differentiator: I can share this. My entire sales team can use the exact same assistant with the exact same knowledge.

**[SCREEN: Team member using the GPT]**

That's the power of Custom GPTs—distributable, consistent, standardized methodology across your organization."

---

## SECTION 6: THE INTEGRATION (1 minute)

**[SCREEN: Four tools shown together with connecting arrows]**

**INSTRUCTOR (V.O.):**
"Now let's see how these work together.

**[SCREEN: Workflow diagram appearing step by step]**

Scenario: Preparing a competitive presentation.

Step one: My 'Market Intel' Gem gathers latest competitor news and market data. Grounded, current, verified.

Step two: My Claude Project 'Competitive Intelligence' synthesizes this with existing battle cards and historical analysis. Deep context, comprehensive.

Step three: My 'Presentation Outline' Skill structures findings into a slide framework. Consistent methodology.

Step four: My Custom GPT generates a shareable battle card for the sales team. Distributable asset.

**[SCREEN: Final output shown]**

Each tool handled what it does best. No redundancy. Maximum efficiency.

This is Squadron Architecture in action."

---

## SECTION 7: MAINTENANCE (45 seconds)

**[SCREEN: Calendar/maintenance checklist]**

**INSTRUCTOR (V.O.):**
"Your Squadron infrastructure needs maintenance.

**[SCREEN: Weekly/Monthly/Quarterly breakdown]**

Weekly: Review conversations for patterns. Update Skills that aren't working.

Monthly: Refresh knowledge files. Audit instructions for relevance.

Quarterly: Evaluate expansion opportunities. Share successful patterns with your team.

**[SCREEN: Warning icon]**

The biggest mistake? Building once and forgetting. Stale knowledge leads to stale outputs. Treat your AI infrastructure like you'd treat any business system—with regular care and feeding."
