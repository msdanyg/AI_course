# Module 8 Flashcards
## Systemizing Intelligence: Projects, Skills, Gems & Custom GPTs

---

### Card 1

**Front:**
What three components make up a Claude Project?

**Back:**
1. **Custom Instructions** - How Claude should behave in this context
2. **Knowledge Files** - Documents Claude can reference (PDFs, CSVs, etc.)
3. **Conversation History** - Past chats that inform future responses

*Think of it as: A dedicated office with standing orders, filing cabinets, and institutional memory.*

---

### Card 2

**Front:**
When should you create a Claude Project vs. using a regular conversation?

**Back:**
**Create a Project when:**
- You'll have 5+ conversations on the same topic
- You need consistent context across sessions
- You have reference documents Claude should access
- The work requires specialized behavior (tone, format, terminology)

**Use regular conversation for:**
- One-off questions
- Quick research tasks
- Experiments and exploration

---

### Card 3

**Front:**
What is a Claude Skill and how does it differ from a Project?

**Back:**
**Skill:** A reusable prompt template invoked with a trigger phrase

**Key Difference:**
- **Projects** = Persistent context (for ongoing work in one domain)
- **Skills** = Portable methodology (repeatable tasks across contexts)

*Best practice: Combine them. Use Skills inside Projects for maximum efficiency.*

---

### Card 4

**Front:**
What are the four elements of an effective Skill?

**Back:**
1. **Clear trigger phrase** - How you'll invoke it ("Quick comp on...")
2. **Structured instructions** - What Claude should do
3. **Input placeholders** - Where your specific content goes
4. **Output format** - How results should be structured

---

### Card 5

**Front:**
When should you use Gemini Gems instead of Claude Projects?

**Back:**
**Use Gemini Gems when you need:**
- Real-time web research with current data
- Native Google Workspace integration (Docs, Sheets, Slides)
- Collaborative scenarios where others need access
- Current information tasks (news, trends, recent data)

*Gems excel at grounded research; Projects excel at deep reasoning with documents.*

---

### Card 6

**Front:**
What is the key differentiator of Custom GPTs vs. other persistence tools?

**Back:**
**Distribution capability**

Custom GPTs can be shared with others, enabling:
- Team-wide access to the same specialized assistant
- Consistent methodology across multiple users
- Standardized workflows throughout an organization

*Projects/Skills/Gems are personal; Custom GPTs are shareable.*

---

### Card 7

**Front:**
What is the "repetition tax" and how do persistence tools eliminate it?

**Back:**
**Repetition tax:** The time spent at the start of every conversation re-establishing context (your role, preferences, terminology, background).

**How persistence tools eliminate it:**
- Custom Instructions remember your preferences
- Knowledge Files contain reference documents
- Conversation History maintains ongoing context
- You start at "expert level" instead of zero

---

### Card 8

**Front:**
What's the recommended maintenance schedule for AI infrastructure?

**Back:**
**Weekly:**
- Review Project conversations for patterns
- Update Skills that aren't working well
- Check Gem outputs for accuracy

**Monthly:**
- Refresh knowledge files with current documents
- Audit Custom Instructions for relevance
- Archive inactive Projects

**Quarterly:**
- Evaluate Projects for expansion
- Share successful patterns with team

---

### Card 9

**Front:**
What are the three Project architecture patterns?

**Back:**
1. **The Client Hub** - One Project per major client/account
   - Account history, contacts, meeting notes, preferences

2. **The Workflow Station** - One Project per recurring workflow
   - "Weekly Competitive Roundup," "Customer Escalation Triage"

3. **The Knowledge Base** - Centralized reference Projects
   - "Product Knowledge," "Brand Voice & Style Guide"
