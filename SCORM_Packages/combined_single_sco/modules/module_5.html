The Sensory System: Giving Claude Eyes and Ears

In Module 4, you learned to make Claude's calculations trustworthy through code execution. But there's another reliability challenge we haven't addressed: Claude is brilliant, but blind.

Part 1: The Blind Strategist Problem

Imagine having access to the world's best strategic advisor. They've studied every business book, analyzed countless case studies and can reason through complex problems with remarkable clarity. There's just one catch: they've been in a sealed room since January 2025, with no access to news, market updates or current events.

!
The Critical Limitation
Claude's knowledge has a training cutoff. Ask about yesterday's news, current pricing or recent product updates, and you'll get either outdated information or confident-sounding guesses. Neither is acceptable for professional work.

This is "The Blind Strategist Problem." Claude can reason powerfully about information you provide, but it cannot:

  • Browse the web or access current information
  • Check today's stock prices, news or weather
  • Verify if a company still exists or what they're currently charging
  • Know what happened in your meeting last week

Part 3: Gemini as Recon & Radar

Grounding with Google Search

Gemini's superpower is "Grounding with Google Search"—the ability to access current web information and cite its sources. When you ask Gemini about a competitor's pricing, it doesn't guess. It searches, finds current information and tells you where it found it.

📡
Why This Matters
Claude would try to answer a competitive question from training data. Gemini can retrieve today's actual pricing page.

The Double Check Feature

Gemini includes a "Double Check" button that highlights which statements are backed by sources. Green highlighting means the claim links to a found source. Orange means the statement couldn't be verified. This helps you distinguish between grounded facts and AI-generated additions.

Part 4: The Fact Brief

When you have verified research from Gemini, you need a structured way to hand it to Claude. The Fact Brief is your formal intelligence report.

Fact Brief Template

Header

Topic: [Subject of research]
Research Date: [Today's date]
Source Tool: Gemini Advanced

Verified Facts

Organized by category, each fact includes:
• The information
• Source URL

Needs Verification

Statements from research that lacked strong source support

Research Gaps

Information you couldn't find but might need

Part 5: Granola as Flight Recorder

Meeting context often determines whether AI output is relevant or generic. Granola captures this context automatically.

📼
Local-First Architecture
Granola processes audio on your Mac—recordings never leave your device. This addresses privacy concerns about cloud-based meeting capture.

Annotation Weighting

When you type notes during a Granola-captured meeting, you're signaling importance. Granola's AI weights sections where you annotated more heavily, surfacing what mattered to you rather than just what was discussed longest.

Key Takeaways

  1. Claude is powerful but blind. It needs you to provide current information and context.
  2. Gemini grounds research in reality. Use it for anything time-sensitive with Double Check verification.
  3. The Fact Brief prevents hallucination. Structured handoff ensures Claude works with verified facts.
  4. Granola captures institutional memory. Meeting context transforms generic advice into tailored strategy.
  5. The Squadron multiplies capability. Each tool does what it's best at—together they're more powerful than any single tool.

Lab Exercise: The Intelligence Pipeline

Duration: 25-30 minutes | Tools: Claude, Gemini

🎯
Objective
Practice the complete Squadron workflow: gather intelligence with Gemini, process context like Granola captures, and synthesize with Claude.

Scenario

You're a Customer Success Manager at ActivTrak preparing for an important client meeting. The client has mentioned they're also evaluating Hubstaff. You need to gather competitive intelligence, process meeting context and create a preparation brief.

Part 1: Gemini Research Mission (10 min)

1

Conduct Competitive Research

Open Gemini (gemini.google.com) and use this research prompt:

Prompt 
I need competitive intelligence on Hubstaff for a client conversation. Please research and provide:

1. Hubstaff's current pricing tiers and what's included in each
2. Their most recent product updates or feature announcements (last 6 months)
3. Key strengths customers mention in reviews
4. Common complaints or weaknesses from customer feedback
5. How they position themselves against competitors

For each fact, note the source. Format as a structured fact brief with clear sections.
2

Verify with Double Check

After receiving the response:

  • Click the Double Check button
  • Note which claims are grounded (green) vs. unverified (orange)
  • Move unverified claims to your "Needs Verification" section

Part 2: Processing Meeting Context (10 min)

3

Review Simulated Meeting Notes

The lab includes sample "Granola-style" meeting notes from previous client conversations. Open a new Claude conversation and use the context extraction prompt from the lab materials to analyze stakeholder dynamics, buying signals and concerns.

Part 3: Squadron Synthesis (10 min)

4

Create Combined Brief

Open a new Claude conversation and combine your Fact Brief with the meeting context analysis. Request a pre-meeting preparation brief with:

  • Situation summary
  • Competitive positioning based on verified facts
  • Stakeholder-specific strategies
  • Objection responses
  • Meeting agenda recommendation

Success Criteria

  • Created a structured Fact Brief from Gemini research
  • Identified which claims are grounded vs. need verification
  • Processed meeting context to extract strategic insights
  • Executed the verified handoff (Fact Brief + Context to Claude)
  • Produced client-ready deliverables based on verified information
