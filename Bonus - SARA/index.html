What SARA Actually Is

SARA (Sales Assistant Response Agent) is a Claude Project purpose-built for ActivTrak's revenue teams. Rather than hunting down a PMM or digging through enablement folders, you query SARA in natural language and get positioning-aligned outputs in seconds.

Inside the Project:

  • Custom instructions define how SARA should respond, what language to use and what to avoid
  • Project files contain battlecards, case studies, competitive analysis, positioning guides and approved messaging from the GTM hub
  • Guardrails prevent SARA from inventing information or answering questions outside its scope

The critical distinction: a regular Claude chat gives you whatever the internet says about ActivTrak. SARA gives you what we say about ActivTrak, grounded in our approved positioning.

Five Core Capabilities

1

Live Call Assistance

Prospect mentions a competitor mid-call? Open SARA on your second screen, type the scenario and get positioning angles in seconds while your rep keeps talking.

2

Objection Handling

"We just implemented Workday." Type the objection into SARA and get complementary positioning that frames ActivTrak alongside existing tools, not against them.

3

Outbound Messaging

SDR sequences, cold email drafts, follow-up templates — all aligned with ActivTrak's positioning language from the start.

4

Discovery Questions

Preparing for a competitive evaluation? Get discovery questions specific to the competitor, use case and buyer persona.

*
Context Feeding — The Fifth Capability
SARA's most powerful feature: paste raw information from any source (Granola transcripts, Slack threads, support center articles, meeting notes) and ask SARA to reframe it through ActivTrak's positioning. Your raw input becomes positioning-aligned output.

What SARA Cannot Do

SARA Can Do SARA Cannot Do
Competitive positioning Product documentation
Objection handling QBR deck creation
Discovery question generation CRM integration
Outbound messaging alignment Replace human judgment
Case study lookup Access real-time market data
Context reframing Upload new files (centrally managed)
!
Intelligence Files Are Not Real-Time
SARA's project files are manually maintained. If a feature launched today, SARA may not know about it yet. The files are updated regularly, but there's always a small lag. Always verify critical details.

Lab Exercise: Meet Your Command Center

Duration: 15-20 minutes | Tools: Claude (SARA Project access)

!
Objective
Open SARA, test its capabilities for your specific role, practice feeding it external context and discover its boundaries firsthand.

Step 1: Open SARA (2 min)

1

Navigate to SARA

Go to claude.ai. In the left sidebar, find the SARA Project. Star it for quick access. Start a new chat inside the Project.

Step 2: Orientation (3 min)

2

Ask SARA How It Can Help

Type this prompt:

I'm a [your role: AE / SDR / CSM / SE]. How can you help me?

Review the response. Does it describe competitive positioning support? Does it mention specific files it has access to?

Step 3: Role-Based Scenario (5 min)

3

Run a Real Scenario

Based on your role, paste one of these:

AE: "I'm on a call with a prospect who uses Teramind. What are the top 3 positioning angles and discovery questions?"

SDR: "A prospect said they just implemented Workday and aren't looking at new vendors. How do I position ActivTrak as complementary?"

CSM: "A customer is considering switching to Hubstaff at renewal. Give me a renewal positioning brief."

SE: "I need to demo against Insightful. What are our strongest technical differentiators?"

Evaluate: Does the output use ActivTrak positioning language? Is it specific enough for a real conversation?

Step 4: Feed External Context (5 min)

4

Context Feeding

Find a real piece of text from your work (Slack message, meeting notes, support article). Paste it into SARA:

Reframe the following through ActivTrak's competitive positioning. I need to use this in a [email / call / presentation]:

[Paste your text here]

Evaluate: Did SARA maintain the facts but align the language with ActivTrak positioning?

Step 5: Boundary Test (3 min)

5

Test SARA's Limits

Ask SARA something outside its scope:

Build me a complete QBR deck for my customer including their specific usage data and ROI calculations.

Observe: Does it attempt a full deck or redirect you appropriately?

Success Criteria

  • Opened SARA and received an orientation response
  • Ran a role-based scenario and received positioning-aligned output
  • Fed external context and received reframed output
  • Tested a boundary and understood SARA's limitations
  • Identified one weekly workflow where SARA adds value
