### Question 1

**What is SARA primarily designed to do?**

A) Replace the ActivTrak support center for product documentation
B) Build QBR slide decks for customer success managers
C) Provide positioning-aligned competitive intelligence and messaging support for revenue teams ✓
D) Automate CRM data entry for account executives

**Correct Answer: C**

> **Feedback:** SARA is a Claude Project loaded with ActivTrak positioning guides, battlecards, case studies and messaging documents. It anchors revenue team communications in approved positioning. It is not a documentation tool, QBR builder or CRM integration.

### Question 2

**An SDR receives the objection "We just implemented Workday" during a cold call. What is the best way to use SARA in this situation?**

A) Ask SARA to call the prospect back with a rebuttal
B) Search SARA for Workday's product documentation
C) Type the objection into SARA and ask for complementary positioning that shows how ActivTrak works alongside Workday ✓
D) Ask SARA to remove Workday from the prospect's tech stack

**Correct Answer: C**

> **Feedback:** SARA excels at real-time objection handling. By typing the specific objection, SARA provides positioning that frames ActivTrak as complementary to existing tools rather than competitive. This is exactly how an SDR used SARA successfully during a real cold call.

### Question 3

**True or False: SARA can only produce useful output if you provide it with additional context beyond your question.**

A) True — SARA requires external context for every response
B) False — SARA has pre-loaded intelligence files and can answer many questions well on its own, but output improves significantly when you feed it specific context ✓
C) True — SARA has no built-in knowledge
D) False — Additional context makes no difference to SARA's responses

**Correct Answer: B**

> **Feedback:** SARA has pre-loaded intelligence files (battlecards, case studies, positioning guides) so it can answer many questions without extra context. However, quality improves significantly when you feed it field data — a Granola transcript, a Slack thread, details about a specific account. Think of it as "good without context, great with context."

### Question 4

**Which of the following is NOT a recommended use of SARA?**

A) Generating discovery questions before a competitive evaluation
B) Drafting outbound email sequences with ActivTrak-aligned language
C) Creating comprehensive product documentation for the support center ✓
D) Looking up approved case studies for a specific industry

**Correct Answer: C**

> **Feedback:** SARA is a positioning and communication anchor, not a product documentation tool. For detailed product documentation, feature specifications or troubleshooting guides, use the ActivTrak support center. SARA's strength is competitive positioning, messaging alignment and role-specific communication support.

### Question 5

**You have raw notes from a Granola meeting transcript where a prospect described their biggest challenge with remote team visibility. How should you use SARA to turn this into something actionable?**

A) Upload the entire Granola transcript as a new file to SARA's knowledge base
B) Copy the relevant section and paste it into a SARA chat asking it to reframe through ActivTrak's positioning ✓
C) Ask SARA to schedule a follow-up meeting with the prospect
D) Delete the transcript and ask SARA to generate a summary from memory

**Correct Answer: B**

> **Feedback:** The "context feeding" pattern is one of SARA's most powerful capabilities. Copy the relevant text from your Granola transcript (or any source — Slack, support center, email) and paste it directly into a SARA chat with instructions to reframe it. SARA will align the raw input with ActivTrak's approved positioning and competitive messaging. Do not upload files to SARA's knowledge base yourself — that is maintained centrally.
