## SECTION 1: WHAT SARA IS (0:45-1:45)

[VISUAL: Screen recording — Claude interface showing the SARA Project]

**PRESENTER:**
SARA lives inside Claude as a shared Project. When you open it, you'll see it in your left sidebar — star it so it stays pinned.

[DEMO: Show SARA Project in sidebar, click to open]

Inside, there are two things doing the heavy lifting.

[VISUAL: Highlight custom instructions area]

First, custom instructions. These tell Claude how to respond: use ActivTrak positioning language, reference our competitive differentiation and frame everything through "Insights, Not Oversight."

[VISUAL: Highlight uploaded files area]

Second, the knowledge base. Battlecards for every major competitor. Case studies by industry. Messaging guides and positioning frameworks. All centrally maintained by product marketing — you don't need to upload anything yourself.

[VISUAL: Presenter on camera]

**PRESENTER:**
Think of it this way: a regular Claude chat knows what the internet says about ActivTrak. SARA knows what *we* say about ActivTrak. That's a critical difference when you're on a call with a prospect.

## SECTION 2: FIVE THINGS SARA CAN DO (1:45-3:30)

[VISUAL: "5 Core Capabilities"]

**PRESENTER:**
Let me walk through five things SARA does well, with real examples from our team.

[VISUAL: "1. Live Call Assistance"]

**PRESENTER:**
Number one: live call support. You're on a call and the prospect mentions Teramind. While the conversation continues, you open SARA on a second screen and type the scenario. Within seconds, you have positioning angles and discovery questions.

[DEMO: Type into SARA: "Prospect is evaluating Teramind. What are our top 3 positioning angles and what discovery questions should I ask?"]

[VISUAL: SARA's response appearing]

Greg on our sales team does this regularly. He said SARA helped spur conversations further and deeper by surfacing questions he wouldn't have thought to ask mid-call.

[VISUAL: "2. Objection Handling"]

**PRESENTER:**
Number two: real-time objection handling. A prospect says they just implemented Workday. Instead of stumbling, you type the objection into SARA and get complementary positioning back instantly.

[DEMO: Type: "Prospect says they just implemented Workday and aren't looking at new vendors. How do I position ActivTrak as complementary?"]

This actually happened. An SDR got a prospect on the phone, typed the Workday objection into SARA and got a full list of how we complement that platform — in real time, during the call.

[VISUAL: "3. Outbound Messaging"]

**PRESENTER:**
Number three: outbound messaging. Need a cold email sequence or follow-up template? SARA writes it using our approved positioning from the start.

[VISUAL: "4. Discovery Questions"]

Number four: discovery question generation. Preparing for a competitive evaluation? Ask SARA for questions tailored to the competitor and the buyer persona.

[VISUAL: "5. Context Feeding"]

**PRESENTER:**
And number five — the most powerful one — context feeding. Copy text from anywhere: a Granola transcript, a Slack thread, a support center article, your own rough notes. Paste it into SARA and ask it to reframe through ActivTrak's positioning.

[DEMO: Paste a short paragraph of raw meeting notes, then type: "Reframe this through ActivTrak's competitive positioning for a follow-up email to the prospect."]

The more context you give SARA, the sharper the output. Good without context. Great with it.

---

## SECTION 3: WHAT SARA CANNOT DO (3:30-4:15)

[VISUAL: "Know the Boundaries"]

**PRESENTER:**
Equally important: what SARA can't do.

SARA is not a product documentation tool. For feature specs and troubleshooting, use the support center. SARA knows how we *talk* about features, not the technical details of how they work.

SARA is not a QBR builder. It won't generate a branded presentation with your customer's usage data.

And SARA does not replace your judgment. It drafts. You review. You send. That's the rule — AI drafts, humans send. You clear missions for takeoff and own what leaves the runway.

One more: SARA's intelligence files are maintained centrally. Don't upload your own files to the Project. If something seems outdated, let product marketing know.

## SECTION 4: USING SARA FOR YOUR ROLE (4:15-5:15)

[VISUAL: Four role cards — AE, SDR, CSM, SE]

**PRESENTER:**
Same Command Center, different mission profiles. Here's a quick-start prompt for each role.

[VISUAL: AE card highlighted]

**AEs:** "I'm evaluating against [competitor]. What are our top three positioning angles?" Use SARA for mid-call intel, discovery questions and proposal language.

[VISUAL: SDR card highlighted]

**SDRs:** "Prospect said [objection]. Give me complementary positioning." Use SARA for cold call recovery and outbound sequences.

[VISUAL: CSM card highlighted]

**CSMs:** "Customer considering [competitor] at renewal. Draft a positioning brief." Use SARA for renewal defense and expansion value stories.

[VISUAL: SE card highlighted]

**SEs:** "I need technical differentiators versus [competitor] for a demo." Use SARA for demo talking points and architecture-level differentiation.

[VISUAL: Presenter on camera]

**PRESENTER:**
And if you're not sure where to start, just open SARA and type: "How can you help me? I'm a [your role]." It'll map its capabilities to your workflow.
