## From Blueprint to Base: Why SARA Exists

In Module 4, you learned how to build your own Mission Headquarters from scratch. You created Claude Projects with custom instructions, uploaded files and established persistent context that eliminates the rebuild tax every time you start a conversation.

But what if a fully-equipped Command Center was already waiting for you? Intelligence files loaded. Standing orders written. Radio frequencies tuned to your exact mission. That's SARA.

**SARA (Sales Assistant Response Agent)** is a Claude Project purpose-built for ActivTrak's revenue teams. It takes everything you learned in Module 4 and shows you what a mature, centrally-maintained Project looks like in production. Rather than hunting down a PMM or digging through enablement folders, you query SARA in natural language and get positioning-aligned, accurate outputs in seconds.

## What SARA Actually Is

SARA is a role-aware prompt framework inside Claude. Think of it as a specialized Mission Command Center where:

- **Custom instructions** define how SARA should respond, what language to use, what to avoid and how to handle questions it can't answer
- **Project files** contain battlecards, case studies, competitive analysis, positioning guides and approved messaging documents from the GTM hub
- **Guardrails** prevent SARA from inventing information, fabricating case studies or answering questions outside its scope

The critical distinction from a regular Claude chat: when you open a new chat and ask about ActivTrak, you get whatever the internet says about the product. When you ask SARA the same question, you get what *we* say about the product, grounded in our approved positioning and competitive intelligence.

**A practical example:** Does ActivTrak support time zones? A generic Claude chat might give you whatever it scraped from the web. SARA will frame the answer through our positioning, using the language we want to use in customer conversations, not what a random blog post says.

### The Squadron Metaphor

If Module 4 taught you to build a hangar, SARA is a hangar that's already built, stocked and staffed. The intelligence files are your pre-loaded mission briefings. The custom instructions are your standing orders. And the connectors — Granola, Slack, Google Drive — are your Field Data Feeds flowing in real-time intelligence from the field.

## What SARA Can Do

SARA excels at five core capabilities, each proven in real-world use by ActivTrak's revenue teams.

### 1. Live Call Assistance

You're on a call. The prospect mentions a competitor. While your rep keeps the conversation going, you open SARA on your second screen and type the competitive scenario. Within seconds, you have positioning angles, differentiation points and suggested questions to steer the conversation.

> *"I asked Claude to let me know the most relevant questions to ask when someone is evaluating Teramind. That helped spur the conversation further and deeper."* — Greg, Sales Manager

### 2. Real-Time Objection Handling

A prospect throws an unexpected objection. Instead of scrambling, type it into SARA and get complementary positioning instantly.

> *"An SDR got a prospect on the phone and they mentioned they just implemented Workday. SARA explained how we're a complementary tool and listed out all the bullet points of how we enhance that platform."* — Jay, SDR Manager

### 3. Outbound Messaging Creation

Need SDR sequences, cold email drafts or follow-up templates? SARA produces messaging that's aligned with ActivTrak's positioning from the start. No more writing in generic language and hoping it sounds right.

### 4. Discovery Question Generation

Preparing for a competitive evaluation? Ask SARA for discovery questions specific to the competitor, the use case or the buyer persona. The questions come pre-loaded with awareness of how ActivTrak positions against each competitor.

### 5. Context Feeding and Reframing

This is SARA's most powerful capability. Take raw information from any source — a Granola meeting transcript, a Slack thread, a support center article, rough notes from a call — paste it into SARA and ask it to reframe through ActivTrak's positioning. Your raw input becomes positioning-aligned output.

## What SARA Cannot Do

Understanding SARA's boundaries is just as important as knowing its capabilities.

**SARA is not a product documentation tool.** For detailed feature specifications, troubleshooting steps or implementation guides, use the support center. SARA knows *how we talk about* features, not necessarily the technical details of *how they work*.

**SARA is not a QBR builder or deck creator.** It can help you refine language on a slide or suggest talking points, but it won't generate a consistent, branded presentation. Use the dedicated POV Project for that.

**SARA does not replace your judgment.** Every output needs human review. AI drafts. Humans send. You clear missions for takeoff and own what leaves the runway.

**SARA's intelligence files are manually maintained.** They are not real-time. If a feature launched today, SARA may not know about it yet. The files are updated regularly, but there's always a small lag.

## How Different Roles Use SARA

Same Command Center, different mission profiles.

**Account Executives** use SARA for mid-call competitive intel, discovery question generation, proposal language refinement and business case framing. When a deal gets competitive, SARA gives you the positioning ammunition without waiting for a PMM.

**SDRs** use SARA for cold call objection handling, outbound sequence creation and persona-aligned messaging. When a prospect drops an unexpected objection, SARA is faster than searching through enablement folders.

**Customer Success Managers** use SARA for renewal positioning, expansion value stories, case study lookup and communication reframing. When a customer is evaluating alternatives at renewal, SARA provides the competitive defense language.

**Sales Engineers** use SARA for technical positioning against competitors, demo talking points and differentiation summaries. When you need to explain why ActivTrak is architecturally different from Teramind, SARA frames it in positioning language, not just feature tables.

## Feeding SARA Field Intelligence

SARA works well with just a question. It works *much better* when you feed it context from the field.

**The pattern is simple:** Copy text from an external source. Paste it into SARA. Tell it what you need.

Sources you can feed SARA:
- **Granola transcripts** — paste meeting notes for follow-up drafting
- **Slack threads** — copy a back-and-forth discussion for a clear, concise summary
- **Support center articles** — paste technical content to get customer-friendly language
- **Email threads** — drop in a customer conversation to draft a positioning-aligned response
- **Raw notes** — your own bullet points, rough thoughts, whatever you have

**Connectors** can also pull information in. If you've enabled the Granola or Slack connectors in Claude, SARA can reach into those sources directly. Not everyone has these set up, and that's fine, copy-paste works just as well.

The principle: **the more context you give SARA, the sharper the output.** A vague question gets a generic answer. A question with pasted context gets a specific, actionable one.

## Getting Started

Two things to do right now:

1. **Star the SARA Project** in Claude so it shows up on your left sidebar. This makes it fast and accessible, not buried in a list.

2. **Ask SARA how it can help you.** Open a new chat in the SARA Project and type: *How can you help me?* If you haven't set personal preferences, add your role: *I'm an SDR. How can you help me?* The response will map SARA's capabilities to your specific workflow.

Think of SARA as an always-available intern who has read every battlecard, every case study and every positioning doc we've ever produced. The intern doesn't know everything, but they're very hardworking, very smart and ready to go the moment you walk in.
