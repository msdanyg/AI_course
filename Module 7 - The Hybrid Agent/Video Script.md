# Module 7 Video Script
## The Hybrid Agent: Mac, Mobile & Docs

**Target Length:** 10-12 minutes
**Format:** Screen recording with voiceover and occasional talking head

---

### OPENING (0:00-0:30)

[SCREEN: Split screen showing iPhone, MacBook, and Google Docs]

**VOICEOVER:**

"Your best ideas don't happen at your desk.

They happen on your morning walk. During your commute. In the gap between meetings.

And by the time you sit down to execute, they're gone.

[PAUSE]

Today, you'll learn to build a Squadron that works everywhere you do."

[SCREEN: Title card - "Module 7: The Hybrid Agent"]

---

### SECTION 1: THE PROBLEM (0:30-2:00)

[SCREEN: Calendar showing back-to-back meetings, iPhone on table, laptop closed]

**VOICEOVER:**

"Here's what the typical knowledge worker's day looks like:

7 AM: Brilliant insight while walking the dog. Make mental note.

8 AM: Rushed morning. Mental note forgotten.

9 AM: Back-to-back meetings. No time to think.

12 PM: Need to draft that proposal. Staring at blank page. The insight from this morning? Gone.

[PAUSE]

This is the **fragmentation problem**.

Your tools don't talk to each other. Your mobile AI is disconnected from your desktop AI. Voice notes sit untranscribed. Screenshots pile up in Photos.

And the intelligence you capture is trapped in the wrong place."

[SCREEN: Show fragmented workflow diagram]

**VOICEOVER:**

"But what if your Squadron could follow you?

What if you could capture raw thinking on your phone, sync instantly to your Mac, and process it with full Claude power—all without friction?

That's what we're building today."

---

### SECTION 2: THE RAMBLE-TO-GOLD WORKFLOW (2:00-4:30)

[SCREEN: iPhone screen recording]

**VOICEOVER:**

"Let me show you. It's 7 AM. I'm walking to my car. I have clarity on how to position ActivTrak for a healthcare client."

[DEMO: Open Claude mobile app, tap microphone]

**VOICEOVER (speaking into phone):**

"Okay, so for Meridian Healthcare, I think we're leading with the wrong angle. They don't care about our dashboard features. What they care about is that three departments are showing burnout risk and they're losing nurses. We should open with their pain—turnover costs 5 million per 100 nurses. Then show how workload balance directly addresses that. The demo becomes about their problem, not our product."

[SCREEN: Claude transcribing in real-time]

**VOICEOVER:**

"I didn't write anything. I didn't organize my thoughts. I just talked.

[PAUSE]

Now I'm at my desk. Watch what happens."

[SCREEN: Switch to Mac, open Claude Desktop]

**VOICEOVER:**

"My conversation synced automatically. Here's my ramble from the car. Now I process it."

[DEMO: Type prompt into Claude]

**PROMPT ON SCREEN:**
```
ROLE: Chief of Staff helping transform voice notes into polished work.

RAW CAPTURE:
[Paste morning ramble]

TASK: Extract core insights, structure logically, draft a polished proposal outline.
```

[SCREEN: Claude generates structured proposal]

**VOICEOVER:**

"In 30 seconds, Claude turned my scattered morning thoughts into a structured proposal:
- Executive summary leading with turnover costs
- Problem statement using their metrics
- Solution mapped to workload balance
- ROI projection
- Implementation timeline

[PAUSE]

From voice note to actionable draft in under 10 minutes. That's Ramble-to-Gold."

### SECTION 3: THE MAC POWER USER (4:30-6:30)

[SCREEN: Mac desktop, Claude Desktop app open]

**VOICEOVER:**

"Now let's talk about why Claude Desktop on Mac is a game-changer.

First: **Universal Drag-and-Drop**."

[DEMO: Drag PDF from Finder into Claude]

**VOICEOVER:**

"I just drag files directly from Finder. No upload button. No browse dialog. Just drag.

Watch this—"

**VOICEOVER:**

"Competitor website screenshot. Drag directly into Claude. Ask for analysis. Instant."

[SCREEN: Claude analyzing competitor positioning]

**VOICEOVER:**

"Second: **Apple Notes as staging area**.

Everything syncs via iCloud. Voice memos I record on iPhone appear on Mac within seconds."

[DEMO: Show iPhone voice memo, then show it transcribed in Mac Notes]

**VOICEOVER:**

"I don't email myself. I don't use third-party sync tools. It just works.

[PAUSE]

This is the Mac Squadron advantage: zero friction between capture and processing."

### SECTION 4: THE HTML BRIDGE (6:30-8:30)

[SCREEN: Claude interface, then Google Docs side-by-side]

**VOICEOVER:**

"Now, the problem everyone hits: the **Copy-Paste Gap**.

You create a perfect table in Claude. You paste it into Google Docs. And—"

[DEMO: Paste Markdown table into Docs, show it breaking]

**VOICEOVER:**

"Disaster. Plain text with pipe characters. No formatting. Unusable.

[PAUSE]

Here's the fix: the **HTML Bridge**."

[SCREEN: Claude chat]

**VOICEOVER (typing prompt):**

"Instead of asking for a Markdown table, I ask for an HTML table with specific CSS."

**PROMPT ON SCREEN:**
```
Create a comparison table: ActivTrak vs Competitor.

Format as HTML table with:
- border-collapse: collapse
- border: 1px solid #333
- Header row: background #f0f0f0
Render directly, not in code block.
```

[SCREEN: Claude generates visual HTML table]

**VOICEOVER:**

"Claude renders the visual table. Now I copy the **rendered version**—not the code—and paste into Docs."

[DEMO: Copy visual table, paste into Docs, show it arriving perfectly formatted]

**VOICEOVER:**

"Perfect. Borders intact. Headers shaded. No manual formatting.

[PAUSE]

This works for complex documents too—headers, lists, tables, all styled with inline CSS."

[SCREEN: Show complex formatted document going from Claude to Docs]

**VOICEOVER:**

"You just eliminated 20 minutes of formatting frustration. That's the HTML Bridge."
