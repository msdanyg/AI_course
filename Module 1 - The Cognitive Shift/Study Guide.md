# Module 1: The Cognitive Shift
## Quick Reference Study Guide

---

## Core Concept

**AI is a probabilistic reasoning engine, not a search engine.** It generates responses by predicting the most likely next word—your job is to constrain those probabilities toward useful outputs.

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Probabilistic Reasoning Engine** | AI system that generates content by predicting probable next tokens based on learned patterns |
| **Water Glass Effect** | Irrelevant context degrades AI reasoning capability |
| **Lost in the Middle** | AI pays less attention to information in the middle of context windows |
| **Solo Pilot** | One person, one AI, one prompt—reactive and inconsistent |
| **Squadron Leader** | Orchestrated AI agents with specialized roles—systematic and reusable |

---

## Squadron Metaphor Mapping

| Tool | Squadron Role | Function |
|------|--------------|----------|
| Claude | Mission Control | Reasoning, strategy, synthesis, writing |
| Gemini | Recon & Radar | Real-time research, fact verification |
| Granola | Flight Recorder | Meeting capture, structured context |

## The Mission Briefing Structure

```
[ROLE] Who the AI should be

[CONTEXT] Background situation

[DATA] Specific information needed

[TASK] What to do (single, clear objective)

[CONSTRAINTS] What NOT to do

[FORMAT] Desired output structure
```

---

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Structure prompts like mission briefings
- Curate context ruthlessly—include only what's needed
- Tell AI what to focus on AND what to ignore
- Use specialized tools for specialized tasks
- Frame analysis as "insight" not "oversight"
- Place instructions at the end of prompts

### Don't
- Dump entire documents without structure
- Ask one AI to research AND write AND critique
- Assume more context = better results
- Use surveillance-oriented framing
- Accept first response without verification
- Mix instructions with data without clear separation

---

## The Framing Test

Before sending a prompt about workforce data, ask:

> "Does this frame the task as **insight** (supportive, systemic, consultative) or **oversight** (surveillance, punitive, individual blame)?"

**Avoid:** "Find who's underperforming"
**Use:** "Identify teams that might need additional support"

---

## Quick Diagnostic

**If AI output is unfocused or off-topic:**
- Check for Water Glass Effect—remove irrelevant context
- Add explicit focus: "Focus only on X. Ignore Y."
- Restructure using mission briefing format

**If AI output is generic:**
- Add specific data and constraints
- Use few-shot examples of desired output
- Specify role/persona more precisely

**If AI contradicts itself or invents facts:**
- Separate research (Gemini) from reasoning (Claude)
- Constrain to provided data: "Use only the information above"
- Verify claims before using
