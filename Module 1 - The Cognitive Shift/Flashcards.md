# Module 1: The Cognitive Shift
## Flashcards

---

### Card 1

**Front:**
What is a "probabilistic reasoning engine"?

**Back:**
An AI system that generates responses by predicting the most likely next word based on patterns learned during training. Unlike a search engine (which retrieves existing content), it creates new content by traversing possible paths through a semantic space. Your job is to constrain those probabilities so the high-probability paths lead to useful outputs.

---

### Card 2

**Front:**
What is the "Water Glass Effect"?

**Back:**
The phenomenon where irrelevant context degrades AI reasoning capability. Named after a riddle where AI models fail not because they lack knowledge, but because distracting details in the prompt scatter their attention away from the core problem. Solution: curate context ruthlessly and explicitly tell the AI what to ignore.

---

### Card 3

**Front:**
What's the difference between a Solo Pilot and a Squadron Leader approach to AI?

**Back:**
**Solo Pilot:** One person, one AI, one prompt. Reactive, improvised, inconsistent results. Everything dumped into a single interaction.

**Squadron Leader:** Commander of specialized AI agents with defined roles. Proactive, systematic, reusable workflows. Breaks complex tasks into coordinated stages with structured handoffs.

---

### Card 4

**Front:**
What is the "Lost in the Middle" phenomenon?

**Back:**
Research finding that AI models pay high attention to the beginning and end of context windows, but attention drops significantly for content in the middle. This means where you place information in your prompt affects how the AI processes it. Put critical instructions at the end, orientation at the beginning, and data in between.

---

### Card 5

**Front:**
What are the four components of a well-structured prompt (mission briefing)?

**Back:**
1. **Opening orientation** - Who you are, what we're doing
2. **Core data payload** - The specific information needed for this task
3. **Clear instructions** - What to do with that data
4. **Output specifications** - Format and constraints for the response

### Card 6

**Front:**
In the Squadron metaphor, what role does each tool play?

**Back:**
- **Mission Control (Claude):** The reasoning engine—strategy, synthesis, writing, complex logic
- **Recon & Radar (Gemini):** The sensory system—real-time research, fact verification, grounding
- **Flight Recorder (Granola):** Context capture—meeting transcription, structured notes, institutional memory

---

### Card 7

**Front:**
Why does asking one AI to research, write, AND critique create an "identity crisis"?

**Back:**
These tasks require conflicting cognitive modes. Research needs skepticism and precision. Creative writing needs flow and narrative risk. Criticism needs harsh detachment. A model optimized for creativity invents "facts." A model constrained to accuracy produces robotic prose. Squadron Leaders assign specialized roles to avoid this conflict.

---

### Card 8

**Front:**
How should ActivTrak employees frame AI prompts to align with "Insights, Not Oversight"?

**Back:**
Frame analysis tasks as support-oriented, not surveillance-oriented.

**Avoid:** "Find who's underperforming"
**Use:** "Identify teams that might need additional support or resources"

The AI follows your framing. Surveillance framing produces surveillance outputs. Insight framing produces consultative recommendations that align with ActivTrak values.
